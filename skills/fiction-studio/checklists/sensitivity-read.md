# Sensitivity Read Checklist

An **optional, author-invited** pass, offered by Virginia around the beta-read
phase (Phase 8). Its purpose is craft and integrity, not censorship: portrayals
that are lazy, stereotyped, or unwittingly harmful also tend to be *weaker
writing* — flatter characters, borrowed clichés, a narrower world. This pass
catches those, and flags places where a real sensitivity reader from the relevant
community would strengthen the work. It surfaces concerns for the author to weigh;
the author decides.

Offer it, don't impose it. If the author declines, note that and move on.

## Representation
- [ ] Characters from any group are individuals first, not representatives of a "type."
- [ ] No identity is reduced to a single trait, accent, or plot function.
- [ ] Marginalized characters have agency and interiority, not just a role in the
      protagonist's arc.
- [ ] The cast's diversity (or deliberate lack of it) is a considered choice.

## Stereotype & trope
- [ ] No reliance on tired or harmful stereotypes for shorthand or humour.
- [ ] Watch for loaded patterns (e.g. the disposable minority character, the
      "magical" helper, villainy coded to an identity) — used knowingly, if at all.
- [ ] Dialect/accent rendering respects the speaker rather than mocking them.

## Authenticity & research
- [ ] Cultural, religious, disability, or lived-experience detail is researched,
      not assumed; specifics are accurate, not generic.
- [ ] Depictions outside the author's experience are handled with care — and flagged
      where a community sensitivity reader would add real value before publication.

## Power & framing
- [ ] The narrative doesn't "punch down" — humour and judgement aren't aimed at
      the vulnerable from above.
- [ ] Difficult subject matter (trauma, violence, abuse) serves the story and the
      character, rather than being used as decoration or shock.
- [ ] The framing/POV doesn't unintentionally endorse a harmful view the book means
      to critique.

## Outcome
- [ ] Concerns are listed plainly with the *why*, ranked by seriousness, each as a
      choice the author can act on, push back on, or decline. The aim is a stronger,
      truer book — not a sanitized one.
