# Prose Quality Checklist

Run during/after drafting and before the final polish, as Max. Apply per scene or
per chapter. These are tendencies to check, not laws — the author's voice wins
ties.

## Scene craft
- [ ] The scene enters late and leaves early — no throat-clearing, no lingering.
- [ ] Something changes: a value turns from start to end.
- [ ] The scene ends on a hook or a beat that pulls the reader onward.
- [ ] POV is consistent; we don't head-hop without intent.

## Show vs tell
- [ ] Emotion is dramatized through action/sensation, not just named.
- [ ] Key information arrives through scene, not info-dump.
- [ ] Description is selective and specific — telling details, not inventories.

## Sentences & rhythm
- [ ] Sentence lengths vary; the rhythm isn't monotonous.
- [ ] No unintentional repetition of words/structures close together.
- [ ] Filter words (saw, felt, noticed, realized) trimmed where they distance us.
- [ ] Adverbs and modifiers earn their place; weak verbs strengthened.
- [ ] Passive voice is a choice, not a default.

## Dialogue (Oscar check)
- [ ] Each character sounds distinct; we could tell them apart unattributed.
- [ ] Subtext is present — characters don't say exactly what they mean.
- [ ] Dialogue does double duty: advances plot *and* reveals character.
- [ ] Speech tags are mostly invisible ("said"); action beats carry the rest.

## Continuity
- [ ] Names, traits, and timeline match the bibles.
- [ ] World rules established earlier are honoured here.

## Reader experience (Virginia check)
- [ ] No passage where a reader would get bored, lost, or disbelieve.
- [ ] The promised emotional payoff actually lands.
