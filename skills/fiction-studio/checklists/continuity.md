# Continuity Checklist

Run during the developmental edit (Phase 7) and again before the final polish
(Phase 9), as Tolkien (world facts) or Max (manuscript facts). On a long
manuscript, continuity errors are the most common and most immersion-breaking
defect — and the cheapest to prevent if the `world-bible.md` continuity log is
kept current. Check the draft *against the bibles*, not against memory.

## Characters
- [ ] Names and spellings are consistent throughout (and against `characters/`).
- [ ] Physical details (eye/hair colour, scars, age, height) never drift.
- [ ] Each character's established voice and vocabulary stays in character.
- [ ] Relationships and their history match the profiles (who knows whom, since when).
- [ ] Skills/knowledge a character has are established before they're used.

## Timeline
- [ ] The sequence of events is internally consistent; no scene contradicts an earlier date.
- [ ] Elapsed time (seasons, ages, travel duration, "three days later") adds up.
- [ ] Day/night, weather, and calendar references are consistent within a stretch.
- [ ] Flashbacks/backstory line up with the present-day timeline.

## World & rules
- [ ] Magic/technology obeys the rules and costs set in `world-bible.md` every time.
- [ ] Geography is stable: distances, directions, and travel times don't contradict.
- [ ] Social rules, titles, and customs established earlier are honoured.
- [ ] Objects/props persist sensibly (a destroyed item doesn't reappear; a gun stays where left).

## Knowledge & information
- [ ] **Who knows what, when** is tracked — no character acts on information they
      couldn't have (the most common plot-logic break).
- [ ] Secrets stay secret until a revealing scene; reveals don't contradict prior scenes.
- [ ] What the reader knows vs. what characters know is controlled on purpose.

## Closing the loop
- [ ] Every contradiction found is fixed *and* the fix is reflected in the
      continuity log, so it doesn't resurface in a later pass.
