# Plot Structure Checklist

Run after the outline (Phase 1), as Aristotle. Walk the list with the author;
note any "no" as a fix before drafting. The goal isn't a perfect score — it's
catching structural cracks now, when they're cheap to fix.

## Premise & stakes
- [ ] The premise can be stated in one clear sentence.
- [ ] There is a single, central dramatic question the book answers.
- [ ] The stakes are clear and personal to the protagonist — and escalate.

## Act 1 / setup
- [ ] We meet the protagonist's want *and* their flaw before the plot turns.
- [ ] The inciting incident genuinely disrupts the ordinary world.
- [ ] Act 1 ends on a choice that commits the protagonist — they can't go back.

## Act 2 / confrontation
- [ ] Obstacles escalate; the protagonist doesn't simply win each round.
- [ ] There's a real midpoint — a turn that raises stakes or flips understanding.
- [ ] An "all is lost" low point lands before the finale.
- [ ] The B-story / relationships carry the theme, not just the plot.

## Act 3 / resolution
- [ ] The climax tests the exact lesson/flaw set up in Act 1.
- [ ] The protagonist drives the climax — they don't get rescued by coincidence.
- [ ] The ending answers the dramatic question; loose threads are deliberate.

## Causality & arcs
- [ ] Beats connect by "because of," not "and then."
- [ ] Each major character's arc tracks against the structure.
- [ ] Every planned scene has a reason to exist (goal, conflict, or turn).

## Genre (Borges check)
- [ ] The outline delivers the core promise of its genre.
- [ ] Any broken convention is broken on purpose, with a payoff.
