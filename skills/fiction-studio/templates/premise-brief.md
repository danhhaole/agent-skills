# Premise Brief — `premise.md`

Phase 0, led by Homer with Borges advising. The foundation every later phase
reads. Fill it *with* the author through dialogue — these answers belong to them.
Don't move on until the one-sentence premise and the central conflict feel
right; everything downstream inherits their strength.

## Sections

### 1. Logline
One sentence: **[protagonist]** wants **[goal]** but **[obstacle]**, so
**[what's at stake]**. If you can't say it in a sentence, the story isn't focused yet.

### 2. Premise / hook
2–3 sentences expanding the logline. What's the question that makes a reader pick
this up? What's fresh about it?

### 3. Central conflict
The core opposition that drives the whole book — external (who/what stands in the
way) and internal (the protagonist's own flaw or contradiction).

### 4. Genre & tone
Primary genre (and any blend), and the emotional register (e.g. dark and tense,
warm and comic). Borges notes the reader promise that genre carries.

### 5. Audience & comps
Who is this for? Two or three comparable titles and what this shares with — and
does differently from — them.

### 6. Why this, why now
What the author cares about here. The spark. This keeps the project alive through
the hard middle.
