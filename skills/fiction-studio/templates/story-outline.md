# Story Outline — `outline.md`

Phase 1, led by Aristotle. Builds on `premise.md`. Use a structure from
`references/craft.md` (default: three-act) as the scaffold — but bend it to the
story, never the reverse. Draft section by section; after each, offer the
elicitation menu before continuing.

## Sections

### 1. Overview
- One-sentence premise (carried from `premise.md`)
- Central dramatic question the book answers
- Chosen structure (three-act / Save the Cat / Hero's Journey / Kishōtenketsu)

### 2. Structure
Map the story to the chosen model. For three-act:

- **Act 1 — Setup:** opening image, ordinary world, the protagonist's want and
  flaw, the **inciting incident**, and the choice that locks them into the story.
- **Act 2A — Rising action:** the new world / pursuit of the goal, the B-story,
  early complications building toward the **midpoint** (a turn that raises stakes
  or flips understanding).
- **Act 2B — Escalation:** consequences tighten, the antagonist presses, leading
  to the **all-is-lost** low point and the protagonist's darkest moment.
- **Act 3 — Resolution:** the turn into the finale, the **climax** that tests the
  lesson, the outcome, and the closing image that answers the opening.

### 3. Character arcs
For each major character: starting flaw/wound → what forces change → resistance →
turning point → end state. The arc should track against the structure above.

### 4. Themes
The central question the book asks (not a moral). How plot and character embody
it. Any recurring image or motif.

### 5. Chapter/scene sketch
A rough sequence of chapters or major scenes — one line each: what happens and
what it changes. This becomes the seed for Scheherazade's scene list.

> Run `checklists/plot-structure.md` before declaring the outline done.
