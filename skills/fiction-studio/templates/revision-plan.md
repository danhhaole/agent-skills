# Revision Plan — `revision-plan.md`

Between beta read (Phase 8) and final polish (Phase 9), led by Max. Raw beta notes
are reactions, not instructions — acting on every one at face value is how a book
gets sanded into mush. This template turns Virginia's reactions (and any other
feedback) into a deliberate, prioritized plan: decide what to change, what to
weigh, and what to leave alone *on purpose*. The author owns every call.

## 1. Triage the notes
Sort every piece of feedback into one of three buckets. A note's bucket is the
author's decision, not the note-giver's.

| Bucket | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Must-fix** | A real problem confirmed — confusion, a logic break, a sag, a broken promise. Change it. |
| **Consider** | A fair point that's a judgement call; weigh against the book's intent. Decide and record the decision. |
| **Decline (with reason)** | Feedback that runs against the deliberate design of the book. Keep as-is; write *why*, so the choice is conscious, not lazy. |

> Look for **patterns**, not just individual notes: if several readers snag at the
> same place (even for different stated reasons), that spot is a real problem —
> trust the snag over the diagnosis. One reader's lone preference carries less weight.

## 2. Map fixes to the manuscript
For each **must-fix** and accepted **consider**, record:
- **Note / issue** — the problem in one line.
- **Where** — scene/chapter(s) affected (cross-check `scene-list.md`).
- **Fix** — the concrete change to make.
- **Ripple** — what else this touches (setups, continuity, later scenes, arcs).

The *ripple* column matters most: changing one scene often breaks a planted
foreshadow or a continuity fact three chapters later. Plan the ripples before cutting.

## 3. Re-check what the revision disturbs
After planning the fixes, before declaring the polish done:
- [ ] Do any changes break a setup→payoff? (re-run `checklists/foreshadowing-payoff.md`)
- [ ] Do any changes introduce a continuity error? (re-run `checklists/continuity.md`)
- [ ] Does any character arc still land after the cuts/changes?
- [ ] Did a fix in one scene create a new sag or hole in an adjacent one?

## 4. Hand to polish
The finalized must-fix + accepted-consider list, with ripples accounted for, is
the work order for Max's Phase 9 polish. The decline list is kept on record so the
same notes don't get re-litigated next pass.
