# Pitch Kit — `pitch-kit.md`

Phase 10, led by Max with Homer (and Borges on comps). The manuscript is done —
this is everything needed to *present* it: to an agent, a publisher, a storefront,
or readers. Each piece is a different distance from the book; write the short ones
last, when you know the whole thing cold. Draft with the author, offer elicitation,
and keep every version honest to the actual book — no promising scenes that aren't there.

## 1. Logline (1 sentence)
The whole book in one line: protagonist + goal + central conflict + stakes.
A test of whether the premise is clear. Example shape:
*"When [inciting event], a [protagonist] must [goal] before [stakes] — but [obstacle]."*

## 2. Back-cover blurb (~150–200 words)
The marketing hook a reader meets first. Sets up the protagonist, the world, the
central conflict, and the stakes — then stops at the question, never the answer.
It sells the *promise* of the book (and signals genre + tone), it does not summarize
the plot. End on the hook, not the ending.

## 3. One-paragraph synopsis (~100–150 words)
The full arc in brief, *including the ending* — for an agent/editor, not the reader.
Beginning, the turn, the climax, how it resolves.

## 4. One-page synopsis (~500–800 words)
The complete story spine, present tense, third person: setup, inciting incident,
escalating complications, midpoint, low point, climax, resolution. Names the major
characters and their arcs. Tells the whole story, ending included. This is the
industry-standard synopsis most agents request.

## 5. Comparable titles (comps)
2–4 published books (ideally recent, in-genre, successful-but-not-mega) that share
DNA with this one. Format: *"[Title] meets [Title]"* or a line each on what they
share. Comps position the book in the market and signal its readership. (Borges
advises here.)

## 6. Metadata
- **Title** (and series name / book number, if any)
- **Genre & sub-genre** + closest shelf
- **Word count** (final)
- **Audience** (age band, reader profile)
- **Content notes** (any flags a reader/buyer should know)

## 7. Query letter (optional — for agent submission)
One page, three short parts:
- **Hook** — the logline expanded to a short paragraph that grips.
- **Mini-synopsis** — ~150 words: setup, conflict, stakes (no ending needed here).
- **Bio & housekeeping** — title, word count, genre, comps, one line on the author.
