# World Bible — `world-bible.md`

Phase 3, led by Tolkien. The canon of record for the story's setting — later
phases check continuity against it. Build only what the story needs: a chamber
drama needs a page; an epic fantasy needs the full bible. Internal consistency
beats volume. Offer elicitation after the rules/systems sections.

## Sections

### 1. Premise of the world
The one thing that makes this world *this* world — the central conceit, and how
it differs from our own.

### 2. Geography & setting
The places the story actually visits, rendered with sensory specifics. A rough
map or place-list. How geography shapes the people who live there.

### 3. Society & culture
Power structures, social order, values, taboos, daily texture. Who has power and
who wants it — the seams where conflict lives.

### 4. Rules & systems
The hard constraints. For fantasy: the **magic system** — what it can do, what it
*costs*, who can use it, its limits. For SF: the **technology** and its rigorously
followed implications. Costs and limits create stakes; unlimited power kills them.

### 5. History & timeline
Only the past that pressures the present story — old wounds, unresolved conflicts,
the events characters still live in the shadow of.

### 6. Naming & language
Conventions so invented names and terms feel of-a-piece (sounds, roots, patterns).
A short glossary of key terms.

### 7. Continuity log
A running list of established facts (distances, dates, who-knows-what) to check
drafts against. Add to this as the manuscript grows. Mirror the hard facts into
`canon.json` (the machine-readable source of truth) so `scripts/continuity_check.py`
can verify them — and update both after each chapter. See `references/qa.md`.
