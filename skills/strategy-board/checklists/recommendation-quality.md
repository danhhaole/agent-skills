# Recommendation Quality Check

*Run by Minto with Drucker before Gate 3 — the last inspection before the
document reaches the executive/board.*

## Structure (Minto)

- [ ] SCQA opens the document in four sentences, and the Question matches
      `brief.md` — drift between them is flagged, not smoothed over.
- [ ] Answer first: the recommendation is a full sentence with the "because"
      in it, on the first screen — not revealed at the end.
- [ ] The key line is 3–4 *claims* (not topics), MECE — no two sections argue
      the same point; together they force the governing thought.
- [ ] Bottom-up test passed: each claim is forced by its evidence. Where it
      isn't, the gap went back to the specialist as an analysis gap.
- [ ] The So-what knife was applied: no section survives that doesn't change
      the decision.

## Honesty (Drucker)

- [ ] Three options appear, compared on identical rows; rejected options get
      an honest line each — the reader can see the choice, not just the winner.
- [ ] **What we will NOT do** is present and concrete (markets forgone,
      projects defunded). No sacrifices = no strategy.
- [ ] **Risks we accept** carries the pre-mortem's survivors, with early
      warnings — not buried in an appendix.
- [ ] Recorded dissent is acknowledged where it exists.
- [ ] "Do nothing / wait" was either one of the options or its exclusion is
      justified, with the cost of delay priced.

## Numbers

- [ ] Every figure traces to `fact-base.md` (`[S#]`/`[A#]`) — the deck is
      where unsourced numbers do the most damage.
- [ ] Load-bearing assumptions (per sensitivity) are named in "What must be
      true" with their monitoring plan.
- [ ] Ranges, not false precision; the downside case is shown alongside the
      base case.
- [ ] No option's case rests more than ~⅓ on "synergies" or "strategic value"
      — or it does, and that is stated as the bet being made.

## The ask

- [ ] The decision requested is precise: amount, authority, people, and the
      date it's needed by.
- [ ] The first 90-day step is named, so approval converts to motion the same
      week.
